Memory Flow — I–V

I

The first time you made something
and didn't show anyone

That was the moment. Not the exhibitions, not the posts. The quiet act of creation witnessed only by the work itself. That was when the artist arrived.

II

The night the page
absorbed something real

There are drawings made from skill, and drawings made from urgency. The second kind leave marks that don't know how to close. You make those.

III

Somewhere between
the sketch and the finished thing

The best work lives there. In the draft that was abandoned. In the almost. In the moment before the hand decided what it meant.

IV

The texture of being
misunderstood with precision

People who understand you quickly understand only the surface. The real ones need time. And even then, they mostly feel it — they don't explain it.

V

A year is not a number.
It is a collection of decisions.

To keep going. To make the next thing. To exist as someone who translates the world into marks. That is the practice. That is who you are.

Some people don't need to be
celebrated.
They need to be
experienced.
